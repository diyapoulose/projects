📐 Sets, Reps & Rest Explained ›
SetsGroups of reps you do in a row
RepsTimes you do the movement
Rest between sets45–90 seconds
Rest between exercises60–120 seconds
💡 TipDon't skip rest — your muscles need time to recover between sets or you lose strength and form on the next one.
🔥 Warm-Up — Why It Matters ›

A proper warm-up raises your heart rate, loosens joints and primes your muscles. Skipping it is the #1 cause of injury.

Duration5–10 min
TypeLight cardio + mobility
IntensityLow — just get warm
💡 TipDynamic stretches (leg swings, arm circles) before exercise. Save static stretches (holding) for after.
🏋️ Progressive Overload ›

Your body adapts to exercise. To keep improving you need to gradually increase the challenge — this is progressive overload.

Add repse.g. 10 → 12 → 15
Add setse.g. 2 → 3 → 4
Reduce rest90s → 60s → 45s
Add weightWhen reps feel easy
💡 TipIncrease by 5–10% at a time. Going too fast leads to injury.
🎯 Rep Ranges by Goal ›
Strength1–5 reps, heavy
Muscle growth6–12 reps, moderate
Endurance15–30 reps, light
Fat loss12–20 reps, circuit
💡 TipThe last 2–3 reps should feel hard. If every rep is easy, increase the challenge.
💥 Common Exercise Mistakes ›

Going too fastLess effective
Not breathingDrops performance
Skipping rest daysOvertraining
💡 TipSlow down. A controlled 3-second descent on a squat builds more muscle than bouncing up and down fast.

🥩 Protein — How Much & Why ›

Protein repairs muscle fibers broken down during exercise. Without enough, you won't recover or grow.

Fat loss goal1.6–2.0g per kg bodyweight
Muscle building2.0–2.4g per kg bodyweight
General health1.2–1.6g per kg bodyweight
TimingSpread across all meals

Good sources:

EggsChickenFish Greek YogurtLentilsTofu Cottage CheesePaneer
🍚 Carbs — Energy, Not the Enemy ›

Carbs are your body's preferred fuel source. Cutting them too low kills workout performance.

Pre-workoutEat carbs 1–2h before
Post-workoutReplenish glycogen stores
Best sourcesComplex, whole foods
RiceOatsSweet Potato BananaWhole WheatQuinoa
💡 TipTime your biggest carb meals around your workouts for best energy and recovery.

Recovery Guide
Where gains actually happen — not in the gym.
😴 Sleep — The #1 Recovery Tool ›
Minimum7 hours per night
Optimal8–9 hours
Growth hormone releaseDeep sleep (3–4h in)
Bad sleep effect–20% strength next day
💡 TipNo phone 30 min before bed. Blue light suppresses melatonin and delays sleep onset by up to 90 minutes.
🧘 Active Recovery Days ›

Rest days don't mean doing nothing. Light movement helps flush out soreness and speeds up recovery.

Walking20–30 min easy
Stretching10–15 min
Yoga / mobilityGreat option
AvoidHeavy lifting on rest days
💡 TipA 20-min walk on rest days can reduce next-day soreness by moving blood through tired muscles.
🩹 Muscle Soreness (DOMS) ›

Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness (DOMS) peaks 24–48h after a workout. It's normal, especially for beginners.

When it peaks24–48h after workout
Duration2–5 days
Relieved byMovement, heat, protein
Sharp/joint painStop — see a doctor
💡 TipDOMS is not a sign of a good workout — just a sign your muscles were challenged in a new way. It fades as you adapt.
🧊 Stretching & Cool-Down ›
Duration5–10 min post-workout
Hold each stretch20–40 seconds
TypeStatic (hold, don't bounce)
Before workoutDynamic only
💡 TipStatic stretching before exercise reduces power output by up to 8%. Always stretch after, not before.
⚠️ Signs of Overtraining ›

Training too much without enough rest leads to overtraining syndrome — your performance actually drops.

Persistent fatigueWarning sign
Mood drops / irritabilityWarning sign
Plateauing strengthWarning sign
Getting sick oftenWarning sign
💡 TipIf you feel worse after 2+ weeks of training, take a full deload week at 50% intensity. Rest is not weakness.
